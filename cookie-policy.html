Cookie & Local Storage Policy

Preamble

This Policy describes how SocialVault, developed by Benashe Softworks, uses local storage technologies, cookies and cache on the user's device.

Article 1 — Definition

A cookie is a small text file stored on a user's device. In an Android application, this includes: SharedPreferences, application cache, local databases and temporary files.

Article 2 — Storage Technologies Used

2.1 SharedPreferences

  • interface preferences (theme, language, display);
  • download settings;
  • status of permissions granted;
  • Premium subscription status (active / inactive), with no financial data.

2.2 Application Cache

  • thumbnail previews of recently viewed media;
  • temporary session data for URL resolution;
  • temporary metadata related to downloads;
  • temporary files generated during a video-to-audio conversion, automatically deleted after the operation.

The cache can be cleared via Settings > Apps > SocialVault > Clear cache.

2.3 Local Database (Room / SQLite)

Used to store download history, entirely local, never synchronized with remote servers.

2.4 Premium subscription data

No financial data is ever stored locally. Only a binary indicator (Premium active/inactive) may be cached.

2.5 Vault Security Data

A cryptographic hash of the Vault PIN/password is stored locally (Android Keystore where possible). This hash:

  • is never transmitted to Benashe Softworks or to any third-party server;
  • cannot be reversed to recover the original PIN or password;
  • is deleted if the Application is uninstalled or the Vault is reset.

Article 3 — Third-Party Advertising Cookies

The free version integrates a third-party advertising network (e.g. Google AdMob), which may use the Android Advertising ID (AAID). The Premium subscription disables advertising entirely.

Ad personalization can be limited via: Google Settings > Data & Privacy > Ads personalization.

Article 4 — Security-Related Cookies

SocialVault does not store any authentication cookies, session tokens, passwords or login credentials for third-party platforms.

No login credentials are ever stored. This is distinct from the Vault lock data described in Article 2.5, which is a device-side security mechanism, not a tracking cookie.

Article 5 — How to Clear SocialVault's Cache

5.1 Via Android Settings

  • Open the device Settings app;
  • Go to Apps;
  • Select SocialVault;
  • Tap Storage, then Clear cache.

5.2 Via Application Settings

The "Clear cache" option is available in SocialVault's Privacy menu.

Article 6 — Local Data Retention Period

  • Application cache: deleted automatically or manually;
  • SharedPreferences: retained as long as the Application is installed;
  • Download history: retained until manually deleted;
  • Downloaded media: retained indefinitely until deleted by the user;
  • Vault lock data: retained until the Vault is reset or the Application is uninstalled;
  • Converter cache files: deleted after each conversion or cache clear.

Article 7 — No Non-Advertising Third-Party Cookies

Apart from advertising networks and Google Play Billing, SocialVault does not integrate any third-party cookies or trackers for profiling purposes.
